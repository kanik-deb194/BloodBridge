📖 How Blood Type Inheritance Works
🧬
ABO System
Each parent contributes one allele. A and B are codominant; O is recessive.
🅰️🅱️
Possible Genotypes
AA/AO = Type A, BB/BO = Type B, AB = Type AB, OO = Type O

Rh Factor
Rh+ (D antigen present) is dominant over Rh−. Population probability applied.

Medical Disclaimer

Help Center

Find answers to common questions about blood donation

How can we help you?
Search through our knowledge base or browse by category
💡 Try "eligibility", "trust score", "badges", or "frequency"
🩸
Who can donate blood?
Anyone aged 18–65, weighing at least 50 kg, with haemoglobin ≥12.5 g/dL, and no recent illness or major surgery within 6 months.
⏱️
How often can I donate?
Whole blood: every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelets: every 7 days, up to 24 times/year. Plasma: every 28 days.
💧
How to prepare for donation?
Drink extra fluids the day before. Eat a healthy meal 2–3 hours before. Avoid fatty foods. Wear comfortable clothing.
📊
What is Trust Score?
Your Trust Score starts at 100 and reflects reliability. It increases for keeping donation promises and decreases for no-shows.
🏅
How do I earn badges?
Badges are automatically awarded when you reach milestones: first donation, 5 donations, maintaining health metrics, etc.
🌍
What is International Rotation?
BloodBridge coordinates with global blood banks. Eligible donors can register to donate during international travel.
🤰
Can I donate if I am pregnant?
No. You must wait at least 6 weeks after delivery before donating blood.
💊
Can I donate while on medication?
Depends on the medication. Common antibiotics require a 2-week wait. Always disclose medications during screening.
